Areas to Avoid

  • Crime Areas
  • Protest Areas
  • Hazard Areas

Nyanga, South Africa

Known for high crime rates, travelers should avoid this neighborhood in Cape Town.

Khayelitsha, South Africa

While Cape Town is beautiful, Khayelitsha is one of its most crime-affected areas.

San Pedro Sula, Honduras

Once considered one of the most dangerous cities in the world with high homicide rates, particularly in certain neighborhoods.

Central District, Caracas, Venezuela

Known for high rates of violent crime, kidnappings, and robbery. Visitors are strongly advised to avoid this area.

Rocinha, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

One of Brazil's largest favelas with ongoing issues of drug trafficking and gang violence despite pacification efforts.

Scampia, Naples, Italy

A neighborhood known for high rates of organized crime activity and drug trafficking controlled by the Camorra crime syndicate.

Petare, Caracas, Venezuela

One of South America's largest slums facing severe issues with gang violence, kidnappings, and drug trafficking.

Zona Norte, Tijuana, Mexico

An area notoriously affected by cartel violence and high homicide rates related to drug trafficking operations.

Kensington, Philadelphia, USA

Known for open-air drug markets and high rates of violent crime associated with the opioid epidemic.

Saint-Denis, Paris, France

A northern suburb with elevated crime rates including drug trafficking, robbery, and assault compared to other parts of Paris.

Tahrir Square, Cairo, Egypt

Historic site of the 2011 revolution that continues to be a flashpoint for political demonstrations and tensions.

Hong Kong, China

Ongoing tensions regarding autonomy and democracy have led to large-scale protests and political uncertainty.

Bangkok, Thailand

Regular political demonstrations occur around government buildings, with tensions between royalist and pro-democracy groups.

Maidan Square, Kyiv, Ukraine

Epicenter of the 2014 revolution and continues to be a gathering point for political demonstrations.

Santiago, Chile

Plaza Italia and surrounding areas regularly experience protests related to economic inequality and constitutional reform.

Beirut, Lebanon

Downtown areas frequently experience demonstrations against government corruption and sectarian political system.

Yangon, Myanmar

Ongoing tensions and demonstrations following the military coup, with potential for sudden security crackdowns.

La Paz, Bolivia

Plaza Murillo and surrounding areas experience frequent political demonstrations related to indigenous rights and governance.

Tehran, Iran

Azadi Square and university areas have been sites of political protests with potential for rapid escalation and security response.

Port-au-Prince, Haiti

Ongoing political instability has led to frequent protests, roadblocks, and security concerns throughout the capital.

Chernobyl Exclusion Zone, Ukraine

While limited tours are available, radiation levels remain dangerous in many areas following the 1986 nuclear disaster.

Death Valley, USA

Extreme summer temperatures regularly exceed 120°F (49°C), creating life-threatening conditions for unprepared visitors.

Mount Sinabung, Indonesia

Frequent volcanic eruptions have created a permanent danger zone around this active volcano in North Sumatra.

Danakil Depression, Ethiopia

One of the hottest places on Earth with toxic gases, acid pools, and extreme conditions that can be fatal without proper guidance.

Fraser Island, Australia

Home to dangerous rip currents, venomous marine stingers, and dingo populations that pose risks to visitors.

Lake Natron, Tanzania

Highly alkaline waters can cause chemical burns on contact and toxic conditions make this lake lethal to most wildlife.

Centralia, Pennsylvania, USA

An underground coal fire burning since 1962 creates toxic gases and unstable ground throughout this nearly abandoned town.

Bikini Atoll, Marshall Islands

Site of nuclear testing with lingering radiation makes certain areas unsafe despite limited tourism to outer portions.

Valley of Death, Kamchatka, Russia

High concentrations of volcanic gases create deadly pockets of air that have killed animals and humans who linger too long.

Lake Nyos, Cameroon

Risk of limnic eruptions releasing deadly CO2 clouds poses a threat despite degassing efforts following the 1986 disaster.
